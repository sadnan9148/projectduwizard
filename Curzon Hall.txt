*******CURZON HALL*********

The Curzon Hall is a British Raj-era building and home of the Faculty of Sciences at the University of Dhaka. 
The building was originally intended to be a town hall and is named after Lord Curzon, the Viceroy of India who
laid its foundation stone in 1904. Upon the establishment of Dacca University in 1921, it became the base of the 
university's science faculty.
Language Movement: The Curzon Hall has attained a great significance in the history of the 
LANGUAGE MOVEMENT. It was here, in 1948, that the students of Dhaka University sounded 
their first refusal to accept Mohammed  Ali Jinnah's declaration that 
Urdu alone would be the state language of the then Pakistan.

Departments: Botany, Zoology, Pharmacy, Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, Soil, Water and 
Environment Science Electrical & Electronics Engineering, Fisheries, Geology, Biomedical Physics & Technology.
Others: A mosque, Agrani Bank, A Science Workshop, Botanical Garden, Canteen.
Nearby Places: Central Field, Doyel Chottor, Mokarram Area, Kabi Sufia Kamal Hall, Dr. Mohamamad Shahidullah HAll,
Fazlul Rahman Muslim Hall.
